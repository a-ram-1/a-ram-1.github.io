Hi, I'm A. I'm a PhD student in Yale's Computational Biology and Bioinformatics program working with Forrest Crawford and Rick Altice, among others. My work aims to curb the incidence of opioid-related overdose deaths in CT by identifying opportunities for enhanced surveillance and means by which harm reduction workers can more impactfully engage high-risk users with comorbidities such as HIV. In the past I've also worked on projects related to trans healthcare.

Before coming to Yale, I did my BS and MEng in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT and did work that was a far cry from public health. You can reach me at a[dot]ram[at]yale[dot]edu. I'm particularly interested in talking to anyone working on matters surrounding harm reduction, incarceration, abolition and social determinants of health.
